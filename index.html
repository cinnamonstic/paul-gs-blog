ABOUT ME

When I'm not studying or working on my latest coding project, you can find me:

MY PROJECTS

LANH4X v2.1
A killer chat program I wrote in C++ for our campus LAN! Works way better than ICQ when you're stuck in the computer lab. Features include private messaging, group chats, and a sweet ASCII art splash screen. My CS professor was actually impressed (shocking!).
Status: COMPLETE - Available for download!

QBasic Casino Deluxe
Remember QBasic? This is a full text-based casino with Blackjack, Poker, and Slots! Complete with sound effects (if you have a Sound Blaster compatible card). Over 500 lines of pure QBasic goodness. Great for killing time during boring lectures.
Status: Beta 0.9 - Still adding Texas Hold'em

WolverineWeb Portal
My attempt at creating the ultimate Michigan web portal! Features local weather (via screen scraping - don't tell anyone!), Detroit news headlines, University of Michigan sports scores, and links to the best Michigan websites. Built with Perl CGI scripts and a MySQL database.
Status: Under Construction - Needs more cowbell

MY WRITINGS & ARTICLES

[12/10/99] Why Windows ME Will Probably Suck
Microsoft is at it again! Based on the beta leaks I've seen, ME (Millennium Edition) is just Windows 98 with more bloatware and even LESS stability. Mark my words - this will be worse than Windows 95 OSR1. Stick with 98 SE or better yet, switch to Linux!

[11/28/99] Open Source is the Future (Even if My Professors Don't Agree)
Had another heated discussion in Systems Analysis class about proprietary vs. open source software. Dr. Peterson thinks Microsoft will dominate forever, but he's WRONG. Linux is growing fast, Apache runs most web servers, and companies are starting to "get it." The revolution is coming!

[11/15/99] Why I Stopped Using Netscape Navigator
After years of loyalty to Netscape, I've finally switched to Internet Explorer 5.0. I hate to admit it, but IE is faster, more stable, and actually renders CSS correctly. Navigator 4.7 crashes too much and Mozilla is still too buggy. Don't tell my Linux friends...

[10/30/99] Building My First E-Commerce Site (What a Nightmare!)
Spent three weeks building an online store for a local comic book shop using Cold Fusion. Between SSL certificates, payment processing, and browser compatibility issues, I'm amazed ANY websites work! But hey, at least I learned a ton about databases.

[10/05/99] Y2K: Hype or Disaster?
Everyone's freaking out about Y2K but honestly? Most modern systems will be fine. Sure, some old COBOL mainframes might hiccup, but your PC isn't going to explode at midnight. Stock up on Mountain Dew instead of canned goods, folks.
